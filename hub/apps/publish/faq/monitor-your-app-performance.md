# Monitor Your App Performance

Learn how to use Partner Center to track performance metrics, analyze crash reports, engage with user feedback, and gain insights to help continuously improve your app.

---

<details>
<summary><strong>How can I track my app’s performance and user engagement in Partner Center?</strong></summary>

Partner Center provides a powerful analytics dashboard that shows how your app is performing after publication. Key reports include:

- **Acquisitions:** Track how many users viewed and installed your app, segmented by source (e.g., Store search, direct links) and region.
- **Usage:** View data on how often users launch your app and how long they engage with it.
- **Add-on performance:** If your app offers in-app purchases, you can monitor how these are being acquired by users.
- **Filters:** Reports can be filtered by date range, market, OS version, and device type to allow for detailed analysis.

Reports can be viewed directly in Partner Center or downloaded as TSV files for offline review. These insights help you understand your app’s adoption, identify trends, and spot potential areas for improvement.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>Where can I see customer reviews of my app and respond to user feedback?</strong></summary>

You can access the **Reviews** report in Partner Center’s analytics section. This report consolidates customer feedback from the Microsoft Store and displays:

- Star ratings and written reviews.
- The ability to filter reviews by region and date.
- A reply feature that allows you to respond directly to reviews from within Partner Center.

Responding to user feedback shows that you value customer input and are actively supporting your app. Keep responses professional and avoid including any personal or sensitive information.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>How do I monitor crashes or performance issues my users encounter?</strong></summary>

The **Health** report in Partner Center tracks your app’s technical reliability. It includes:

- Crash counts and unresponsiveness rates.
- Diagnostic details such as stack traces and impacted OS/device combinations.
- Error trends over time.

This data helps you quickly identify bugs or stability issues, allowing you to prioritize and resolve problems in future updates. Reviewing health data regularly is essential for maintaining a stable, high-quality app experience.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What kind of insights does Partner Center provide to help improve my app?</strong></summary>

Partner Center’s **Insights** report automatically highlights notable trends and anomalies in your app’s performance data. Examples include:

- Spikes or drops in acquisition or crash rates over the past 30 days.
- Alerts about significant changes that could indicate bugs or successful updates.

If you’ve instrumented custom telemetry in your app, those events will also be reflected in the usage data. These insights help you react quickly to problems and understand the impact of new features, updates, or marketing campaigns.

</details>
